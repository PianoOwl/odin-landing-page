Levels of doneness

This is a rare steak. Considered by many to be undercooked. Quite chewy, and barely warm in the center. An acquired taste.
This is a medium-rare steak. Widely considered to be the most correct way to eat steak. Hot, tender, fatty deliciousness.
This is a medium-well steak. A little overdone, with some grey in the edges, but still juicy and delicious.
This is a well-done hockey pu... ehm... steak. A complete abomination in every sense of the word. Truly tragic.

Well-done steak? Are you kidding me? That's like turning a beautiful piece of art into a pile of ash. A true chef knows that a steak should be seared to perfection on a high heat, then left to rest like a boss, so the juices can redistribute throughout the meat. Serving it well-done is an insult to the cow and a crime against good food. If that's what you want, go to a fast food joint, but don't expect any respect from me. -Gordon Ramsay
